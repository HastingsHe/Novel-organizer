Never Let Me Go - Novel Organizer

Setting Characters Themes Important Moments Symbols Questions

Setting

Hailsham School

Significance: Hailsham is the boarding school where Kathy, Ruth, and Tommy grow up. It’s a seemingly idyllic place but with a dark undercurrent, designed to prepare them for their roles as organ donors.

Details:

  • Hailsham fosters creativity and encourages students to submit artwork for "The Gallery."
  • Focus on physical health but no clear explanation as to why.
  • Isolated from the outside world, a setting that creates both safety and foreboding.

Quotes:

"It was a beautiful place. It was in the countryside, with large playing fields, woods, and a pond." (p. 6)
"Your art will reveal your soul." (p. 26) – Miss Emily.

Impact: The setting influences the characters' identity formation and moral dilemmas, which gradually unfold as they become aware of their true purpose in life.

Characters

Kathy H.

Key Traits: Reflective, empathetic, observant.

Development: Kathy narrates her own story, often revisiting her memories with Tommy and Ruth. She’s a carer who reflects on her past and her complex relationships.

Important Moments: Kathy reflects on her childhood relationships, particularly with Tommy and Ruth, and her role as a carer.

"I’m not trying to boast, but I’ve been a carer now for over eleven years." (p. 3)

Ruth

Key Traits: Charismatic, manipulative, competitive.

Development: Ruth displays an early desire for control, particularly in her relationships with Kathy and Tommy.

Important Moments: Ruth’s competitive nature shows when she lies about receiving a special pencil case, asserting dominance over her peers.

"It’s not what she says, but what she doesn’t say, that puts you in your place." (p. 18)

Tommy

Key Traits: Sensitive, impulsive, misunderstood.

Development: Tommy’s frustrations about his lack of artistic talent are central to his emotional struggles.

Important Moments: Miss Lucy’s words to Tommy about his artistic talent show how different the clones’ fates might be from their expectations.

"She said we weren’t being taught enough about some things. But she didn’t go into specifics." (p. 29)

Themes

Identity and Purpose

Theme Description: The students struggle with their identities and what their purpose is in the world.

Example: The art they produce, particularly in “The Gallery,” is meant to express who they are, but the true purpose of their lives is hidden from them until later.

"Your art will reveal your soul." (p. 26)

Mortality and Acceptance

Theme Description: The inevitable fate of the characters as organ donors looms over them.

Example: Miss Lucy’s cryptic comments about their future point to the novel’s underlying exploration of mortality.

"You were brought into this world for a purpose, and your futures, all of them, have been decided." (p. 35)

Important Moments

The Gallery and Artwork

Significance: Hailsham’s emphasis on art as a form of expression shapes the characters’ understanding of their identities.

Details: Miss Emily claims the students’ art is a representation of their souls, yet they are not told the true reason for the gallery’s existence.

Literary Techniques: Symbolism of art representing identity and foreshadowing of their fate.

Tommy’s Outburst

Significance: Tommy’s emotional outbursts reveal his inner conflict and frustration with the world around him.

Details: His temper and emotional volatility contrast with the calm demeanor expected by Hailsham.

"Tommy’s tantrums were often spectacular." (p. 22)

Symbols and Motifs

The Tape

Significance: Represents Kathy’s longing for connection and a reminder of her childhood.

Details: Kathy’s search for a tape she had with Tommy signifies the bond they share, despite the passage of time.

"We all knew how much it meant to her, and Tommy especially was determined to find it again." (p. 41)

The Woods

Significance: Symbolizes fear and the unknown. The students are warned against the woods but don’t fully understand why.

Questions

  • How does the seemingly idyllic environment of Hailsham contrast with its true purpose?
  • What role does art play in shaping the students’ understanding of their identity?
  • How do Miss Lucy’s warnings challenge the Hailsham ethos?
  • In what ways do the dynamics between Kathy, Ruth, and Tommy foreshadow their future relationships?
