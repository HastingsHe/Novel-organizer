Never Let Me Go - Novel Organizer

Setting

Key locations: Hailsham, The Cottages, and Norfolk. These locations are central to the plot and theme. Hailsham represents the sheltered, controlled lives of the students, while The Cottages and Norfolk highlight their growing awareness of their fate and desire for independence.

Characters

Kathy

Kathy, the protagonist and narrator, is introspective, empathetic, and reflective. Her role as a carer later in life makes her both an observer and participant in the lives of Tommy and Ruth.

Tommy

Tommy is passionate and emotional. He struggles with his identity and his relationship with Kathy and Ruth. His character represents the struggle of understanding one’s purpose and existence.

Ruth

Ruth is manipulative and insecure, but also deeply caring in her way. She often tries to control situations, especially concerning Kathy and Tommy's relationships.

Themes

Memory and Identity

The novel explores the role of memory in shaping identity, particularly through Kathy’s reflective narration. She constantly revisits her memories of Hailsham, trying to make sense of her past.

Mortality and Ethics of Science

The ethical dilemma surrounding organ donation and human clones is central to the story. The characters face the inescapable reality of their purpose and the morality of their creation.

Important Moments

One significant moment occurs when Kathy, Tommy, and Ruth realize the true purpose of their lives as organ donors, which sets the stage for the emotional and ethical struggles that follow.

Symbols and Motifs

The Boat

The boat symbolizes the idea of escape and freedom, especially for Kathy and Tommy. It represents their longing for something beyond their prescribed fate.
