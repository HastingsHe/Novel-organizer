Never Let Me Go - Novel Organizer

Setting

  • Hailsham: Represents innocence and control.
    “It was possible for Hailsham students to leave, to be expelled, or to be ‘disappeared.’”
    (p. 45)
  • The Cottages: A transition toward reality.
    “It was at the Cottages that we first began to have proper conversations about what we were in the larger world.”
    (p. 122)
  • The Boat: Symbolizes decay and mortality.
    “The boat had been abandoned for years, slowly being consumed by the marsh.”
    (p. 191)

Characters

  • Kathy H. - Reflective, empathetic, resilient.
    “I suppose I’ve always known, in some vague way, that we would end up here.”
    (p. 67)
  • Tommy - Emotional, naive, creative.
    “Tommy’s art was his way of proving he had a soul.”
    (p. 81)
  • Ruth - Ambitious, manipulative, insecure.
    “I kept you and Tommy apart because I was afraid. I wanted something just for myself.”
    (p. 144)

Themes

  • Memory and Nostalgia:
    “Memory can be a strange thing. It doesn’t work like I thought it did when I was younger.”
    (p. 67)
  • Ethics of Science:
    “You were brought into this world for a purpose, and your futures, all of them, have been decided.”
    (p. 81)
  • Mortality:
    “It’s what we’re supposed to do... part of our lives.”
    (p. 104)

Important Moments

  • Miss Lucy’s Revelation: Shatters students’ illusions of a normal life.
    “You have to know who you are and what you’re meant to do.”
    (p. 95)
  • Ruth’s Apology: Encourages Kathy and Tommy’s relationship.
    “I’m sorry. I made things difficult for both of you.”
    (p. 201)

Questions

  • What does it mean to have a soul in the context of the novel?
  • How do art and creativity help the characters assert their humanity?
  • How reliable is Kathy as a narrator, and how does her perspective shape the story?
