Never Let Me Go - Novel Organizer

Setting Characters Themes Important Moments Symbols & Motifs

Setting

Hailsham School [Bookmark]

Hailsham is an elite boarding school in the English countryside, emphasizing health, creativity, and a mysterious “special” purpose.

"We were all very special, and we’d been told, but somehow we never quite made the connection." (p. 5)

The Playing Fields: A space where children bond but also experience subtle tensions. It reflects both freedom and control.

Characters

Kathy H.

Role: The narrator. Reflective and observant.

Development: Through Kathy’s narration, we learn about her close yet complex relationships with Ruth and Tommy.

"I'm not trying to boast, but I’ve been a carer now for over eleven years..." (p. 1)

Tommy

Role: An outsider with temper issues, often teased by others.

"The tantrum Tommy threw in the playing field that afternoon was something else." (p. 9)

Ruth

Role: Kathy’s close but domineering friend. She often controls their social dynamics.

"Ruth’s version of events always had to be the one that counted." (p. 12)

Themes

Memory and Identity: The narrative structure shows Kathy reflecting on past events, highlighting how memories shape her identity.

"Memory is what defines us, Kathy, it ties our past to our present." (p. 15)

Ethics of Science: Through subtle clues, the story raises questions about the moral implications of cloning and human rights.

Important Moments

Tommy’s Tantrum: An early defining moment that showcases Tommy's vulnerability and the underlying pressures of Hailsham.

"Miss Lucy had her own views about how we should be taught." (p. 18)

Ruth’s Power Play: Ruth excludes Kathy from a social activity, showing her controlling nature and setting the stage for their relationship dynamics.

Symbols & Motifs

The Tape: A symbol of personal identity and connection for Kathy. Losing and finding it again signifies the fragility of their world.

"I kept that tape as long as I could, but Ruth wanted me to share it." (p. 20)
